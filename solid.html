What is SOLID?

Solid is one of the four fundamental states of matter (the others being liquid, gas, and plasma). The molecules in a solid are closely packed
together and contain the least amount of kinetic energy. A solid is characterized by structural rigidity and resistance to a force applied to the
surface. Unlike a liquid, a solid object does not flow to take on the shape of its container, nor does it expand to fill the entire available
volume like a gas. The atoms in a solid are bound to each other, either in a regular geometric lattice (crystalline solids, which include
metals and ordinary ice), or irregularly (an amorphous solid such as common window glass). Solids cannot be compressed with little pressure
